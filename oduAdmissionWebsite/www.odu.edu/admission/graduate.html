Meet a Few of Our Students

Doctoral Candidate, Delaram

Delaram received her M.S. in Experimental Psychology at ODU and is now a fourth-year doctoral student in the Educational Psychology and Program Evaluation program. She would like to become a faculty member at a research institution in an educational psychology department after graduation.

Awards: AERA Division C Outstanding Poster Award, Spring 2018, The Annual Meeting of the American Educational Research Association, New York, NY; APS Student Research Award, May 2016, The 28th Annual Convention of the Association for Psychological Sciences, Chicago, IL

Research Assistantship: National Institutes of Health Grant in Educational Psychology and Program Evaluation

Research Topic: Why STEM students have a higher dropout rate than students in other programs

Why ODU: "I chose to pursue my advanced degrees at ODU because of the research the faculty were doing combined with my interest in assisting in it."

Favorite Experiences as a Student: "Interacting with and being supported by my department, department chairs, the faculty, and advisors. Everyone is so supportive and accessible!"

Engineering Management and Systems Engineering Ph.D. Candidate, Ying

Ying holds an M.E.M. in Master of Engineering Management and Systems Engineering, and is pursuing his Ph.D. in the same field. He's a very versatile student with experience in both academia and industry, and enjoys teaching and working as an industry consultant.

Awards: Intercultural Relations Award, 2015 Student Engagement and Enrollment Services

International Journal of Systems Engineering

Applications of Management Science (Chapter, Volume 19)

Research Interests: Decision making in complex systems management and problem structuring methods

Why ODU: "I learned of ODU's excellent graduate program in my field while working as a barista in Washington, D.C. I decided to earn my master's degree and decided to stay for my Ph.D. because of the diverse student body and innovative research projects with the faculty."

Favorite Experiences as a Student: "Having my first publication definitely tops my list! I didn't know that was even possible but with the help from faculty, it became a reality!"

Higher Education Ph.D. Candidate, Courtney

Courtney is pursuing her Ph.D. in Higher Education with hopes of either working as a faculty member within an education or psychology department at a research institution. Overall, she wants a career that provides lots of student contact!

Graduate Assistantship: Department of Higher Education

Research Interest: Job satisfaction for adjunct faculty and organizational commitment

Why ODU: "My interactions with ODU professor and graduate program director, Dr. Chris Glass, is the reason I chose ODU. He clearly conveyed the program and how it could incorporate my clinical psychology background."

Favorite Experiences as a Graduate Student: "I love my peers and classmates! We keep each other motivated, and enjoy spending time of the classroom as well. The program's flexibility, meeting with policy stakeholders in Washington D.C., and getting to know the faculty are also some of my favorites."
