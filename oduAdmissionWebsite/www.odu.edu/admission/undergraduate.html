Transfer student

Transfer students are defined as those who have attended another 2 or 4-year institition and are looking to transfer credits to ODU to earn a bachelor's degree.

Course by Course Evaluation Tool Transfer Information Request Information Apply for Admission How and When to Apply

Non-degree student

ODU offers non-degree admission to visiting students or those interested in receiving licensure for any academic term. Non-degree students must complete an online application for admission into the university and are not eligible to receive financial aid until they become a degree-seeking student.

> Learn more

Non-Degree Eligibility

  • Students who are under academic suspension from Old Dominion University or any other institution are not eligible to enter as a non-degree student.
  • Traditional freshman applicants who were denied admission to Old Dominion University are not eligible for non-degree admission without a change from the original application type.

Second degree student

Second degree students have already earned a bachelor's degree from a regionally accredited college or university and want to pursue an additional bachelor's degree in a different field of study. Second degree applicants apply for a specific program and applications are reviewed by the staff from the intended program.

Returning student seeking reactivation

Reactivation is for students who have previously attended Old Dominion University on a degree-seeking basis and then voluntarily left the University in good academic standing. Reactivation is only required for students who have been separated from the ODU for more than one full academic year. Reactivation is NOT for students who left the university because of academic suspension, non-degree students or international students. Second degree students who reactivate must return to the original program of study.
